SQ3R Study Method

An effective approach to actively reading and studying academic materials

1 Survey
2 Question
3 Read
4 Recite
5 Review

1. Survey (Scan the Section)

Quickly scan the section to get an overview. Focus on headings, subheadings, bold terms, diagrams, and summaries.

What are the main headings or themes in this section?

Which key terms or concepts stand out?

What do the images, graphs, or diagrams suggest about the content?

2. Question (Generate Questions Before Reading)

Turn the headings and key points into questions to guide your reading.

What is the main idea or purpose of this section?

How does this information connect to what I already know?

Why is this concept important or how can it be applied?

3. Read (Read Actively to Answer Your Questions)

Read the section carefully, looking for answers to your questions.

4. Recite (Summarize and Recall Key Points)

Without looking at the text, write down or say aloud what you remember.

What are the three most important points from this section?

Can I explain the concept in my own words?

What examples or analogies help me understand this better?

5. Review (Reflect and Reinforce Learning)

Go back over the section and your notes to reinforce understanding.

Did I answer all my initial questions accurately?

What gaps remain in my understanding?

What connections can I make to other topics or real-world applications?

Review Tip: Consider revisiting this section in 24 hours and then again in a week to strengthen long-term retention.
